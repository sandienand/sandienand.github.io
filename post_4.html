The Integrity Protocol Framework

Published: September 1, 2025 by The Integrity Protocol

WIP theory I hope to evolve. At the heart of ethical governance—whether in AI, data, or digital systems—lies a simple triad:

1. Hold Only What You Must

Principle: Minimise collection and retention.

Why: Every piece of data you collect becomes a liability as much as an asset. The less you hold, the smaller the attack surface, the lower the compliance burden, and the greater the respect for individual autonomy.

Application: In data privacy, this means collecting only the information essential to deliver a service. In governance, it means avoiding the construction of systems that hoard "just in case" information.

2. Secure What You Hold

Principle: Responsibility does not end at collection; it begins there.

Why: Trust depends on stewardship. Failing to secure what you've taken stewardship of—data, algorithms, or decisions—means betraying both your users and your mandate.
